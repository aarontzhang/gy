# Aaron Zhang — Life Context

## Early Years (Wayland, MA)

Born and raised in Wayland, Massachusetts. From a young age, drawn to building and creating things — had an entire room of Legos (thousands of dollars worth) and built 3D foam puzzles of cathedrals, skyscrapers, and other architectural structures. Naturally talented at drawing, sketching, and painting, but couldn't follow structured art classes because he needed to create on his own terms. Played violin for roughly nine years and won an award around third grade for strong performance.

Elementary school was a struggle — frequent trips to the principal's office. Not an academic problem but a behavioral one: too independent-minded to sit still in a system that wasn't challenging enough.

## Middle School — Meadowbrook

Parents identified the core issue as under-stimulation and enrolled him in Meadowbrook, a highly selective private middle school — 12% of his graduating class would go on to attend Harvard. Strong first year, then discovered video games and everything unraveled. Started sneaking his laptop at night after parents confiscated it. Sleep suffered, grades cratered.

During this time, was tested and diagnosed with ADHD. Never used the extra time accommodations — reasoned that if he couldn't focus for 90 minutes, adding 45 more wouldn't help. The diagnosis didn't change his self-perception or sense of capability.

## New Hampton School (2 Years, Boarding)

Attended New Hampton, a private boarding school. First year was excellent — approximately a 4.0 GPA. Then COVID hit. Built a gaming PC the week before leaving campus and got deeply addicted to Fortnite. Reached top 200 in the US. Learned video editing with the idea of making tutorials, but never followed through — it was more of a passing dream than a real pursuit. Wanted to go pro at Fortnite; in hindsight, probably delusional, but the competitive drive was real.

Second year was entirely online and grades dropped significantly.

## New England Innovation Academy (NEIA) — The Turning Point

Transferred to a brand-new school focused on startups and entrepreneurship. It was originally recommended to his brother Alvin (a strong traditional student), but when their parents visited, they immediately recognized it as the right fit for Aaron. Admission was untraditional — involved designing a class rather than a standard interview.

Class of about 100 students. This was the first school where Aaron was a consistently good student for the entire duration. The peer group made the difference.

**Student government:** Ran for class president, won, and established the student government. Quickly realized the role itself wasn't for him, but gained real experience in managing people and driving execution.

### 10th Grade — Soap Company (First Real Business)

In chemistry, learned to make soap using lye and salts. A startup class assignment required selling 10 units of a kitchen product. Aaron's team sold over 1,000 units instead.

- Designed and manufactured the soap themselves, iterating heavily on the production process.
- Sold at school, farmers' markets, and online.
- Started a TikTok account that grew to ~15K followers and ~1M views.
- Total revenue: approximately $10–15K.

First real experience making money from something he built end-to-end. This was the moment entrepreneurship went from concept to conviction. The company was eventually sold to the school as an educational vehicle — it still runs today as a club where students apply to work there, continue marketing on the original social media accounts, sell at farmers' markets, and gain real startup experience.

### AutoCut — AI Video Editor

Built an AI video editor where you describe edits in plain language and the AI handles them. Indexes video using visual and audio cues, then executes cuts, captions, and removals via FFMPEG. Stack includes Supabase, Claude (for parsing edit requests into FFMPEG commands), and OpenAI Whisper for audio transcription.

Origin: directly born from the YouTube experience. Was making $3,500/month but couldn't sustain the editing load alongside school. AutoCut solves that exact pain point. Thesis: content is splitting into hyper-optimized viral video vs. authentic value-driven content. The second group doesn't need complex software — they need something that handles the same 3–4 edits every video. "Canva beat Adobe Illustrator by doing less, better." Competitors like Descript, Runway, and CapCut AI are all building for professional editors, not casual creators.

## What Matters for Ideation

**Licensed personal trainer.** Goes to the gym every day — not out of discipline, but because he wants to.

**Core philosophy:** "I only work on things I genuinely want to work on." Everything flows from intrinsic motivation, not obligation. This applies to school, projects, and what he chooses to build.

A few patterns that run through everything:

1. **Builder instinct** — Legos to foam puzzles to soap manufacturing to apps to AI tools. The medium keeps changing; the compulsion to make things doesn't.
2. **Hyperfocus as a feature** — ADHD means when something clicks, he goes all-in. Games, Fortnite rankings, soap sales, coding sprints. The challenge is picking the right target.
3. **Novelty-seeking** — Thrived in the first year at every school, then needed new stimulation. Aware of this pattern. Implications for what kind of work sustains his energy long-term.
4. **Entrepreneurship is native** — Not something learned in a class. Parents are founders, first business at 15, VC exposure since childhood.
5. **AI-native thinking** — Doesn't just use AI tools — thinks in terms of what AI makes newly possible. Automates everything. Builds with AI as a core assumption, not an add-on.
6. **Taste for design** — From drawing and painting to product design to UI. Cares about how things look and feel, not just whether they work.
7. **Competitive edge** — Top 200 Fortnite, #1 Clash of Clans, $130K portfolio, 1000 soap units on a 10-unit assignment. Doesn't just participate — tries to win.

## Startup Preferences

- B2B over B2C. Wants to build something with real moat — network effects or data, not just a better wrapper.
- Interested in unglamorous, overlooked industries where big money flows but few tech people are looking (concrete, agriculture, industrial services, etc.).
- "Swallow the company" thesis: don't sell software to SMBs — replace the entire SMB with an AI-native version.
- Distribution and UX are the durable moats as technical barriers drop. Deeply believes in this.
- Avoid: hard tech, medical, finance/crypto, GPT wrappers, anything the big labs will absorb in 1-2 model iterations.
- Agency model is viable as a starting wedge — deliver the result before the tech is fully there — but avoid ones requiring heavy client approval loops (e.g., branding/ad creative).
- Has a co-founder with CS/engineering background. Not building an engineering-focused company.
- Attracted to being the most technically innovative company in an unglamorous industry — the "nobody's thinking about this" factor matters. VCs finding the problem novel is a strong signal.
- Video content (short and long-form) is a guaranteed part of the go-to-market. Both founders want to make videos.
- Venture-scale ambitions. EF fellowship alum — $250K/8% investment is a potential option but not confirmed. Also considering YC, NEO, and other accelerators.
- No defense/weapons companies. Wants to build something good for society.
- Looking for non-obvious ideas — the "wait, that's actually brilliant, how did I never think of that?" factor. Not incremental improvements to known problems.
